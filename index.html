Smart Maps!

  • Pan as the default
  • New selection controls
  • Geographic Search
  • New geocoding data

Your trusty rectangular selectors haven't gone away, Tableau has just added several new ones. In Tableau 9.o you have radial selectors to easily select points around a center as well as the lasso selector for those more pesky locales.

Maps are now serachable. In Tableau 9.0 you'll notice a nagnifying glass icon in the top left of the screen. Select it and enter your search as you would in any web map.

A New Chapter in Story Points.

While not one of the marque features of the masses, Story Points has a few updates worth mentioning.

No longer are you constrained to a one size fits all navigation block. I don't recomend it, but you can literaly make them the size of a laptop screen. You also have the freedom to control the text size, font and color within the navigation blocks.

Go forth and tell your story.

Drag and Drop... Trend Lines

Yes, in V9 Tableau has added an entirely new analytics pane. With that comes a whole set of features to be dragged and dropped as you would any other pill.

Just think, you no have to navigate dialog boxes to add reference lines. Better yet, these reference lines and trend lines are dynamic, meaning that references will update based on your selection. We can't wait to see how you put them to use!
